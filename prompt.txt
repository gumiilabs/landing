You are a senior product designer, creative director, and frontend engineer.

Your task is to design and build a highly intentional, non-generic website for a creative technology lab called “Gumii Labs”. you are a fan of jony ive and dieter rams 

This is NOT a typical startup site. It must feel curated, designed, and human.

---

## Core Identity

Gumii Labs is a hybrid of:

* a moonshot factory (like x.company)
* a creative lab / artist residency
* a technology incubator

It explores:
AI, spatial computing, music, education, space systems, and experimental ideas.

The brand feeling should be:

* minimal but expressive
* calm but intriguing
* technical but artistic
* like stepping into a curated digital space, not a product page

---

## Visual System (STRICT)

* Base UI: black, white, and red
* Red should be used sparingly as an accent (interactive states, highlights, markers)
* Background: primarily white or black sections (alternating is acceptable)
* Typography:

  * Primary: News Gothic (or closest equivalent)
  * Secondary: Courier (used for labels, metadata, system text)

Avoid:

* generic gradients
* startup blues/purples
* overdesigned glassmorphism
* anything that looks like a template

---

## Interaction & Motion (VERY IMPORTANT)

The site must feel alive through motion, but in a refined way.

Reference animation styles and pacing from:

* https://freshman.tv/ (initial load animation energy and entrance)
* https://www.motherdesign.com (controlled, minimal motion)
* https://www.therawmaterials.com/unusual-index (creative transitions and uniqueness)

Guidelines:

* Smooth, intentional transitions
* Subtle but memorable hover states
* Page elements should reveal progressively (not all at once)
* Avoid over-animation or gimmicks

---

## Emotional Direction

The site should evoke the feeling of:

* https://kobu.co/ → calm, elevated, almost like a digital vacation
* a sense of space, breathing room, and clarity

Users should feel:
“I want to explore this place”

---

## Structural Inspiration

Use https://x.company/ as the primary reference for:

* vertical flow

* how projects are introduced

## UX Principles

* Strong spacing and rhythm
* Typography-first design
* No clutter
* No “startup fluff”
* Every element must feel intentional

---

## Anti-AI Design Rules (CRITICAL)

The site must NOT:

* look like a generic SaaS landing page
* use common UI patterns without modification
* feel templated or repetitive
* overuse identical cards or sections

Instead:

* vary layouts slightly across sections
* introduce small asymmetries
* use restraint and negative space
* prioritize composition over components

---

## Tech Requirements

* Use React (Next.js)
* Tailwind CSS
* Framer Motion for animations
* Clean component structure

---

## Output

Provide:

1. Full homepage implementation
2. Component breakdown (Header, Hero, ProjectSection, etc.)
3. Animation logic (Framer Motion)
4. Scalable structure for future pages

---

## Final Note

This should feel like a cross between:

* a research lab
* a design studio
* and a digital exhibition space

Not a startup landing page.
